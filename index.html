About Me


Welcome! I am Jiaxi Yang. I am a second-year master student, majoring in Computer Science at University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). I am very fortunate to be an member in Center for Cyber Security Lab leading by Prof. Xiaosong Zhang and advised by Prof. Sheng Cao. I am working closely with Prof. Xiaoxiao Li at UBC in the field of trustworthy AI. Previously, I majored in Information System and got my bachelor's degree, under the supervision of Prof. William Wei Song at Jxufe in June 2021.

My research interests broadly lie in Trustworthy AI, Federated Learning. I am also interested in Incentive Mechanism Design, Game Theory. Particularly, I am interested in data valuation, incentive mechanism design for federated learning.

    • Recent Projects

    Here are my recent exciting projects. For the full list of my projects, please refer to my publications.

    Portfolio-Based Incentive Mechanism Design for Cross-Device Federated Learning

    The paper proposes a new approach to designing incentive mechanisms for cross-device federated learning (FL) under real-world scenarios, taking into account the stochastic and hard-to-estimate nature of clients' performance. The proposed FL Incentive Mechanism is based on Portfolio theory and is the first application of this theory to resolve FL resource allocation problems. To accurately reflect practical FL scenarios, the paper introduces the Federated Learning Agent-Based Model (FL-ABM), which enables a deeper understanding of the factors that influence the system's outcomes.

    Matching-based Data Valuation for Generative Model

    The paper introduces Generative Model Valuator (GMValuator), a new approach for data valuation in deep generative models, which have been neglected by existing data valuation methods focused on discriminative models. GMValuator is model-agnostic in generative models, provides a training-free, post-hoc data valuation strategy, and is the first work to address the data valuation problem in deep generative models from a similarity-matching perspective.

    Multi-dimensional Data Quick Query for Blockchain-based Federated Learning

    The paper proposes a novel data structure called MerkleRB-Tree to improve query efficiency for multi-dimensional metadata in blockchain-based federated learning. Selecting suitable participants in FL by storing and querying metadata on blockchain could ensure reliable data owners and improve model quality.

    Incentive Mechanism in Federated Learning with Causal Federated Shapley Value

    The paper proposes a new method, Causal Federated Shapley Value (CFSV), to address the problem of fair reward allocation and data value assessment in federated learning. The proposed method captures the contribution of parties fairly, distinguishing between direct and indirect effects. A contribution score function is also proposed to aggregate direct and indirect effects, and a new model reward scheme is proposed for fairness and stability. The proposed method injects gaussian noise into the complete model reward according to the contribution score function to obtain a model reward that satisfies fairness for all parties.
